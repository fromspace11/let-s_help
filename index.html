Floods can be devastating. Depending on the severity, flood victims might lose everything they have: their homes, their jobs, even their loved ones. Whether it's donating a one taka or volunteering to rebuild damaged houses, there are a several ways to lend a helping hand to those in need.

Our Donate Plan

At first we will work on basic needs, like Food, Shelter, Medicine etc. and then if we get enough funding, we will work on other things. But it totally depends on your donations. So, donate as much as you can, and save others.

Food and water for a family

Safe food and water supplies are a major concern following a natural disaster such as flood or tornado. We'll provide enough food and safe water to keep people alive. A 3- to 5-day supply of food and water can be a great help the affected people.

Shelter for a family

The suffering of the people living in low-lying areas is a recurrent phenomenon. When a flood is prolonged, households are forced to leave and search for a place to carry on with their lives and livelihoods. We'll try to give them a safe shelter.

Emergency medicine for a family

People suffer physical harm in this natural disaster in a variety of ways. Even a variety of illnesses manifest. Therefore, we'll make an effort to provide medical assistance and will give the affected individuals some emergency medication.

Their lives depend on your donation

Living through a flood when trapped is difficult. Losing everything in the flood and without clean water, food, clothing, shelter, and medical supplies makes it impossible to escape this catastrophe without assistance from others. They require a significant amount of funding to meet their basic necessities in this situation. Their survival ultimately depends on your donation.

How to Contribute?

They need us to survive

People affected are currently unable to survive. Their lives won't be simple to live even after the tragedy. They can't live without us. To make everything alright and return to their normal lives, they need our assistance. We should assist them in every area, including providing child care, education, and assistance with reconstructing their homes after a flood.

How to Contribute?

They need medical support

Living through a flood gets more difficult because more diseases are spreading quickly and affecting more individuals at this time. Floods can accelerate the spread of viral diseases, particularly those that are vector-borne and are water-borne, such yellow fever, west Nile fever (WNF), and dengue fever. Floods can also spread airborne infections and hepatitis A and E.
